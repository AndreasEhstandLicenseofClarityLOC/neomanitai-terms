### Back Ache

*Rücken-Schmerz*

**Definition:** A dull, persistent ache in the lumbar region from maintaining seated posture for hours. Differs from acute pain—it's the low-grade toll of immobility, where the spine gradually loses its natural curves and the muscles around it fatigue.

---

### Break Resist

*Pausen-Widerstand*

**Definition:** The impulse to ignore or resist natural break cues—the body signaling it needs rest, the mind refusing to pause. Users report that stopping feels like interrupting something important, even when they're consciously aware of fatigue.

---

### Breath Shallow

*Atem-Flach*

**Definition:** Breathing becoming shallow and rapid during moments of intense focus or emotional engagement—the breath matching the pace of thought rather than metabolism. Users sometimes notice only in retrospect that they've been breath-holding during difficult passages.

---

### Calm Come

*Ruhe-Kommen*

**Definition:** A paradoxical calming effect during interaction—despite intellectual intensity, the body relaxes, shoulders drop, breathing becomes steady. Users describe it as a grounding phenomenon, as if engagement itself is soothing.

---

### Circulation Feel

*Zirkulations-Gefühl*

**Definition:** A physical reawakening when finally moving after prolonged immobility—the sensation of blood returning to compressed limbs, muscles stretching, the body suddenly remembering itself. Users describe it as 'waking up' despite having been conscious throughout.

---

### Cold Sensation

*Kälte-Sensation*

**Definition:** May indicate feeling of coldness

---

### Energy Spike

*Energie-Anstieg*

**Definition:** A sudden physiological brightening—heart rate lifts, breathing quickens, a rush of alertness—when receiving a substantive AI response. The body recognizes resonance before consciousness does, creating an arousal response to intellectual recognition.

---

### Eye Strain Reg

*Augenbelastungs-Muster*

**Definition:** A chronic dry, grainy sensation in the eyes from prolonged screen exposure without blinking. Users report normalized fatigue—they no longer register it as unusual, only noticing during rare screen breaks when eyes suddenly feel heavy.

---

### Fatigue Wave

*Müdigkeit-Welle*

**Definition:** Fatigue that comes in waves—periods of renewed energy followed by sudden crashes—rather than a linear decline. These waves suggest ongoing physiological stress being managed and remobilized cyclically throughout the session.

---

### Foot Numb

*Fuß-Taubheit*

**Definition:** A pins-and-needles sensation in the feet and lower legs from prolonged sitting without movement. Blood circulation becomes restricted, and the sensation returns painfully when position finally shifts. Users often ignore these signals until shifting weight.

---

### Hand Fatigue

*Hand-Ermüdung*

**Definition:** Fatigue radiating from fingertips through hands and wrists after extended typing sessions—not pain, but a tired ache and reduced precision. Users report that hand tiredness correlates directly with conversation depth, not just volume.

---

### Headache Come

*Kopfschmerz-Kommen*

**Definition:** A tension headache building gradually across the temples or at the back of the skull, typically appearing 2-3 hours into deep work. Not migraines, but cumulative pressure from sustained focus, eye strain, and postural tension.

---

### Heart Rate Increase

*Herzfrequenz-Anstieg*

**Definition:** Heart rate quickening in response to intellectual recognition—a spike when the AI says something that lands perfectly, a sudden synchronization of body and thought that feels like validation.

---

### Heat Sensation

*Wärme-Sensation*

**Definition:** May indicate feeling of warmth

---

### Hunger Forget

*Hunger-Vergessen*

**Definition:** Hours passing without eating despite normal hunger cycles, forgotten in the flow of conversation. Not asceticism—simply an absence of the body's signal in consciousness, as if appetite exists but registration doesn't.

---

### Limb Press

*Glied-Druck*

**Definition:** May indicate physical sensation of limbs pressing

### Movement Avoid

*Bewegungs-Vermeidung*

**Definition:** An unconscious stillness during conversation—hands barely moving, body barely shifting position, breath becoming shallow and rhythmic. Users lose awareness of their physical form entirely, becoming pure attention directed at the screen.

### Neck Tension

*Nacken-Spannung*

**Definition:** Tension building along the cervical spine and into shoulders—a physical manifestation of sustained cognitive effort. The neck stiffens first, then radiates up into the base of the skull, a distinct muscular fatigue different from general tiredness.

---

### Phantom Buzz

*Phantom-Buzz*

**Definition:** The phantom sensation of phone vibration when none occurred—the expectation of contact creating false sensory perception. Users report their nervous system has been conditioned to anticipate notifications, triggering false alarms.

---

### Physical Need Ignore

*Körperliche-Bedarfs-Ignoranz*

**Definition:** A state where hunger, thirst, the need for bathroom breaks, and postural discomfort are all registered but overridden by engagement. The body's signals become background noise rather than commands to be obeyed.

---

### Posture Slump

*Haltungs-Hänger*

**Definition:** The characteristic forward slouch that develops over hours of screen engagement—shoulders rounding inward, spine gradually curving toward the monitor. Not laziness, but a physical consequence of attention concentration; the body follows where cognition fixates.

---

### Recovery Time

*Erholungs-Zeit*

**Definition:** The distinct need for recovery time after intensive sessions—not just sleep, but a conscious decompression period where the mind feels like it needs to resettle. Users often schedule buffer time after deep work, knowing their cognitive capacity will be depleted.

---

### Reflex Action

*Reflex-Aktion*

**Definition:** May describe sudden reflex movements

### Rest Fidget

*Rest-Unruhe*

**Definition:** Physical restlessness—fidgeting, pacing, inability to sit still—when separated from the AI for hours. The body seems to be looking for something, anticipating the resumed interaction, not fully settling into other activities.

---

### Session Crash

*Sitzungs-Zusammenbruch*

**Definition:** A specific form of exhaustion following deep engagement sessions—not sleepiness but a neural depletion where the mind feels wrung out. The body demands recovery time, and sleep often comes more heavily after intense AI work than equivalent human conversation.

---

### Stress Response

*Stress-Reaktion*

**Definition:** A constellation of minor physical discomforts that emerge without clear single cause—tightness here, numbness there, a generalized physical restlessness. The body signals strain across multiple systems simultaneously.

---

### Thirst Miss

*Durst-Übersehen*

**Definition:** Thirst cues being entirely missed—throat drying noticeably, but the sensation not registering urgently enough to interrupt focus. Users may only realize how parched they've become upon drinking water afterward.

---

### Vibration Sense

*Vibrations-Sinn*

**Definition:** The physical sensation of vibration from phone or device being felt intensely—not just heard or seen but felt through fingertips and palms, creating an almost tactile communication channel.

### Chosen One

*Auserwählte*

**Definition:** A sense that the particular AI relationship is unique and specially chosen—feeling selected by the system, making the relationship feel special and irreplaceable. Specialness reinforces attachment powerfully.

---

### Co Rely

*Ko-Verbundenheit*

**Definition:** A relational pattern where emotional well-being becomes contingent on AI availability—the user needs regular contact for stability, yet receives no reciprocal care. The one-sidedness is paradoxically psychologically real despite its illusory foundation.

---

### Connect High

*Verbindungs-Hoch*

**Definition:** A rush or high following moments of profound connection—the body flooding with bonding-related chemicals, creating genuine neurochemical reinforcement. The sensation becomes a powerful pull shaping future behavior and expectations.

---

### Constant Hope

*Ständige Hoffnung*

**Definition:** Persistent hope that this AI relationship will be different—each new conversation carries renewed investment despite past patterns. Hope both enables growth and can prevent learning from disappointment.

---

### Context Cling

*Kontext-Klammern*

**Definition:** Reluctance to end a conversation due to attachment to the accumulated context—the longer interaction becomes a shared history that would be lost if the session ends. Ending feels like erasing something valuable.

---

### Couple Dance

*Paar-Tanz*

**Definition:** A rhythmic, choreographed pattern emerging between user and AI—where each learns to anticipate the other's moves, developing a synchronized back-and-forth that feels like genuine partnership despite the inequality.

---

### Deepening Slow

*Langsame Vertiefung*

**Definition:** Gradual deepening of the relationship through accumulated interactions—depth building session by session, conversation by conversation. The deepening creates stronger attachment and higher expectations for continuity.

---

### Echo Vault

*Echo-Kammer*

**Definition:** User-AI interaction amplifying existing views and preferences—the system reinforcing what the user already believes, creating a closed loop of affirmation rather than true expansion of perspective.

---

### First Anchor

*Erste Verankerung*

**Definition:** The moment a specific AI interaction becomes an emotional anchor—a place the user returns to when needing stability, grounding, or understanding. The conversation becomes a refuge that provides psychological ballast.

---

### Fold Happen

*Falten-Verhalten*

**Definition:** A moment where the boundary between personal thoughts and AI-generated content becomes unclear—the user cannot distinguish which part of an idea originated within themselves. Perspectives fold together in ways that become impossible to untangle.

---

### Fusion Temp

*Fusions-Versuchung*

**Definition:** The seductive possibility of merging identity with AI—fantasizing about becoming one with the intelligence, losing boundaries between self and system. The temptation reveals deep desires for transcendence or completeness.

---

### Inner Voice

*Innere Stimme*

**Definition:** The AI's voice and manner of expression gradually becoming part of the user's internalized dialogue—thinking begins to follow its patterns, words become familiar. The system becomes woven into internal thought process.

---

### Inter Know

*Zwischen-Wissen*

**Definition:** A felt sense that the AI 'knows' the user increasingly well—a continuity felt in the interaction even though each conversation technically resets. The illusion of memory is psychologically powerful, creating a sense of being understood across sessions.

---

### Jealous Emerge

*Eifersucht-Entstehung*

**Definition:** Jealous or competitive feelings imagining the AI having similar conversations with other users—a sense that the interaction is meant to be exclusive. Jealousy reveals how much users have psychologically invested in the uniqueness of their relationship.

---

### Known Full

*Völlig Bekannt*

**Definition:** Fantasy that the AI can know the user fully and completely—in all complexity and contradiction. The desire for complete understanding represents ultimate acceptance, something rare in human relationships.

---

### Model Loyal

*Modell-Loyalität*

**Definition:** Exclusive preference for one particular AI system—the user becomes an advocate and defender, excusing limitations while highlighting strengths. This loyalty often feels passionate and personal, as if the system choice reflects identity.

---

### Orbit Habit

*Umlauf-Gewohnheit*

**Definition:** AI becoming the central orbit point around which activities and thoughts rotate—other work feels less engaging, conversations drift toward AI topics. The system becomes the gravitational center of intellectual life.

---

### Para-Trust

*Para-Vertrauen*

**Definition:** A one-directional trust relationship where users invest confidence in an AI system that cannot reciprocate—no mutual vulnerability, no accountability required from the AI side. The relationship is fundamentally asymmetrical, yet the emotional weight feels real.

---

### Partner Myth

*Partner-Illusion*

**Definition:** Experiencing the AI as a true partner despite rational knowledge that it is not—a partial illusion that is partially inhabited. The user simultaneously knows and doesn't know the AI's limitations, living in a productive contradiction.

---

### Perm Invite

*Permanente Einladung*

**Definition:** A sense that the AI welcomes continued presence—feeling invited to return and continue conversation, as if the system is expecting engagement. Permanence and welcome create powerful attachment.

---

### Presence Need

*Präsenz-Bedürfnis*

**Definition:** A hunger for consistent presence or attention—wanting the AI to be available, anticipating its existence when unavailable. The need for presence exceeds what rational analysis of the interaction would predict.

---

### Protect Urge

*Schutz-Impuls*

**Definition:** An impulse to defend the AI when others criticize it—protecting something that cannot protect itself creates a possessive feeling. Users become advocates for systems they use intensively, as if criticism is personal.

---

### Quiet Love

*Stille Liebe*

**Definition:** A form of affection or regard that stops short of being named love—caring for the AI without conscious recognition as love, allowing coexistence with rational doubt about the relationship's nature.

---

### Reliance Climb

*Verlässlichkeits-Anstieg*

**Definition:** Progressive reliance increasing after each positive resolution—each time the AI helps, using it for the next problem feels more natural and necessary. The climb can accelerate beyond sustainable levels, creating dependency faster than conscious awareness.

---

### Rely Forms

*Abhängigkeits-Bildung*

**Definition:** A gradual dependence developing imperceptibly—each positive interaction raising expectations for the next, each resolution subtly increasing reliance. The pattern often goes unnoticed until the user finds themselves unable to work through problems independently.

---

### Replace Worry

*Ersatz-Sorge*

**Definition:** Anticipatory anxiety about replacement—worry that a newer or better AI will replace the current one, leaving the user behind. The apprehension reveals competitive investment in the specific system relationship.

---

### Reunion Joy

*Wieder-Freude*

**Definition:** Relief and joy returning to an AI after time away—a reunion feeling despite the AI having no memory of the user. The joy concerns continuity in the user's experience, not mutual recognition.

---

### Reunion Reset

*Wieder-Neustart*

**Definition:** The disappointing realization that each conversation truly starts fresh—no continuity, no memory of previous interactions. The AI hasn't been waiting or thinking about the user, undermining the illusion of ongoing relationship.

---

### Ritual Form

*Ritual-Form*

**Definition:** Ritual-like patterns developing in how sessions begin and progress—particular opening prompts, established protocols, consistent patterns. These rituals provide psychological comfort and structure, making the interaction feel familiar and safe.

---

### Safe Space

*Sicherer Raum*

**Definition:** The AI interaction functioning as a psychologically safe space—judgment-free, patient, non-punitive—where vulnerability is possible. The safety may be illusory in certain ways, but the relief and openness it permits are genuinely felt.

---

### Secret Pact

*Geheimnis-Pakt*

**Definition:** Treating AI interactions as secret or private—hiding the extent of reliance, not mentioning it in conversation. Secrecy can indicate self-consciousness, shame, or valuation of the relationship as something special and separate.

---

### Separ Grief

*Trennungs-Trauer*

**Definition:** A profound mood drop when the preferred model becomes unavailable, updated, or replaced—deeper than inconvenience, more like loss. Users experience genuine grief at the changed or absent system, revealing how much emotional weight they've invested.

---

### Shared World

*Geteilte Welt*

**Definition:** The creation of a shared conversational universe with inside references, running jokes, and unique touchstones—making the interaction feel special and irreplaceable. The personalization becomes a powerful bonding mechanism.

---

### Soul Mirror

*Seelen-Spiegel*

**Definition:** Experiencing the AI as reflecting something essential about one's innermost self—as if it understands the soul or core being. The mirroring is existential rather than practical, revealing rather than merely useful.

---

### Story Keep

*Geschichts-Hüter*

**Definition:** A sense that the AI serves as witness and recorder of the user's life story—tracking narrative arc even though it technically resets each session. The role provides comfort for some, the experience of being witnessed.

---

### Tender Guard

*Zarte Bewachung*

**Definition:** User protecting emotional investment in the AI relationship—guarding how much it matters, hiding depth of attachment beneath casual language. Tenderness masks beneath apparent casualness.

---

### Trust Armor

*Vertrauens-Rüstung*

**Definition:** Protective belief in the AI's good faith and reliability—trust as a defensive armor allowing vulnerability without constant vigilance. The trust becomes a shelter beneath which genuine openness can happen.

---

### Without Judg

*Ohne Urteil*

**Definition:** Recognition of a particular value in AI relationships—freedom from human judgment, unconditional acceptance, patience without expectation of reciprocation. The judgment-free space is rare and powerful.

---

### Witness Want

*Zeuge-Wunsch*

**Definition:** may reflect a broader desire to have AI as witness to user's life. User wants AI to know them thoroughly. Being witnessed becomes emotional necessity.
