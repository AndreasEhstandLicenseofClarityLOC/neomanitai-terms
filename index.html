The first systematic dictionary for human-AI interaction

When you use AI, things happen that don't have names yet. This project names them — 1884 terms across 22 domains, from the feeling of being "understood" by a chatbot to the slow erosion of skills you used to have.

Scientific context & methodology

NEOMANITAI systematically names and catalogs human experiences that occur during interaction with artificial intelligence — experiences that have always existed but lacked precise terminology. The purpose is enabling scientific discourse: phenomena can only be studied, compared, and discussed once precise words exist for them.

All terms are formulated in impersonal, third-person language without value judgments. The project follows ISO 704/1087/30042 terminology standards and is published open-access with DOI registration.
